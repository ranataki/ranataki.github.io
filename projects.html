CS248B: FUNDAMENTALS OF CHARACTER ANIMATION PROJECTS

Throughout CS248B, I developed three sophisticated gaming projects that deepened my understanding of advanced physics simulation and computer graphics. Starting with a blob dynamics simulator, progressing to a physics-based pinball game, and culminating in a character animation system, each project challenged me to implement complex mathematical concepts into practical applications. These projects enhanced my expertise in various technical areas, including Signed Distance Functions (SDFs), efficient collision detection for large-scale object interactions, character kinematics using Jacobian matrices, and advanced physics simulations incorporating ray casting, penalty forces, compression mechanics, and area forces. The final project explored space-time motion, bringing together all these elements. This hands-on experience transformed theoretical classroom concepts into functional, interactive gaming applications, bridging the gap between academic theory and practical game development.

POMELO

Pomelo is an innovative teaching assistant robot designed to make learning computer science fundamentals fun and tangible for elementary school students. Through hands-on activities involving block-stacking and maze puzzles, students learn the basics of algorithmic thinking. As children arrange physical blocks to create pathways through mazes, Pomelo brings their solutions to life by using Aruco markers (special visual codes) to read and follow their block-based instructions. This interactive approach transforms abstract programming concepts into a physical, engaging learning experience that complements traditional classroom instruction.
